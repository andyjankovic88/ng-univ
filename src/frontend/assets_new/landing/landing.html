Student Services Staff

Student Services can create Service Pages to increase awareness, improve access and support students more easily

  • Students following Service Pages are notified when staff post resources, announcements and events
  • Drop-in times and events are highlighted to students at the times they occur
  • Support queries can be posted directly to Service Pages - especially useful for online students and local students with full timetables
  • Students receive posts and access resources posted on Support Pages anytime, anywhere with UCROO's mobile applications
Orientation & Transition Staff

UCROO helps Orientation & Transition Staff build a greater sense of community and support throughout the crucial first year of university

  • Start facilitating friendships between enrolment & orientation (high attrition zone)
  • Connect international students still overseas, regional students yet to arrive on campus & remote students with local peers
  • Create events for first year activities and see who has joined them
  • Pin important posts for greater attention & post videos/photos from events
  • Create polls to check-in with students and automatically monitor important first year topics
  • Target posts to first-years based on campus, faculty, international / domestic & study mode
Communications Staff

Communications Staff can use UCROO to better connect students with important information, announcements and events

  • Target posts to students based on campus, faculty, mode of study, international/domestic and year level
  • Pin important announcements and resources to the top of large student groups for greater attention
  • Post polls to check-in and gather feedback from the student body
  • Easily post videos, links, articles and photos with communications
  • Create and highlight university events and see who has joined them
  • University emails are often unopened - comms on UCROO appear in full in students' Recent Activity
  • Comms on UCROO can be posted as push notifications to native mobile applications
  • More students see university comms made on UCROO compared to Facebook Pages

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to commonly asked questions about UCROO below. If you can't find an answer to your question, contact us at support@ucroo.com and we'll be happy to help.

  • What is UCROO?

    UCROO is a private, secure and controlled social network designed for universities and its students.

    For students, UCROO is a university technology that meets contemporary expectations and better connects them with peers, classmates, mentors, instructors, clubs & societies and student services from enrolment to graduation.

    For universities, UCROO is an innovative tool for improving student experience and retention, which offers enterprise level privacy, security, control and analytics not available in other social networks.

  • How can I get a demonstration of the UCROO product?

    You can request a demonstration of UCROO from the Request a Demo tab at the top of the page.

  • What is required to set up a university's UCROO network?

    Very little information is required to set up your university's UCROO network.

    Lists of the following are required:

    • Subjects
    • Degrees
    • Campuses
    • Faculties

    Should the university wish to brand its UCROO network, we will require the university's logo and colours.

    Once this information is provided, the university's UCROO network can be set up within a few days.

  • Do graduates or past staff members count towards the UCROO subscription fee?

    No.

    When a student is no longer enrolled at a university due to attrition or graduation, that user is considered inactive and is no longer counted towards the university's UCROO subscription fee.

    Likewise, when a staff member no longer works at the university, that user's account is considered inactive and is no longer counted towards the university's UCROO subscription.

  • How does UCROO limit access to ensure a university network is private?

    Australia

    When users create a UCROO account, they are required to verify themselves as a student or staff member of their university via the Australian Access Federation.

    Only those students and staff entering valid university credentials are given access to the respective university's UCROO network.

    Outside Australia

    Only students and staff with a verified university email address can access your university network.

    After creating an account, a user must verify the account from their university email before they are given access to the university's UCROO network.

  • Where is UCROO hosted?

    UCROO is cloud hosted at the Rackspace Data Centre in Sydney, Australia.

    Rackspace is the fastest growing cloud hosting provider, servicing over 200,000 companies worldwide.

    For more information, visit Rackspace.

  • What level of control does a university have over its UCROO network?

    UCROO allows universities to brand their network with its logo and colours.

    Universities also have the option to add or remove entire features to suit their network needs.

    Furthermore, universities subscribed to UCROO have access to an Administration Control Centre for their network.

    The Administration Control Centre includes advanced moderation features such as reported/deleted content archives, group management, key word monitoring and usage policy set-up to ensure a high-level of administrative control.

  • How does UCROO collect user profile information?

    Generally, a user provides all user profile information during account creation.

    However, the university may provide this information when a student opts-in to UCROO if the necessary system integrations have been implemented.

    See: 'What integrations are possible with UCROO?' for further integration information.

  • What support is available to students?

    Dedicated email support is provided to students. All student support enquiries lodged are addressed within 4 business hours.

    Students can easily lodge support enquiries from their Account Menu.

  • How does UCROO help ensure uptake of the university's network?

    New initiatives succeed when you choose tools that students genuinely want to use - UCROO has been built from the ground up to drive voluntary adoption and maximise engagement of your university network.

    We believe in a seamless user experience and deliver a quality experience across all devices in order to cater to 21st century expectations.

    Furthermore, an experienced and dedicated UCROO Support Team Member works with the university to determine the best means for creating awareness and engagement with UCROO based on best practice case studies.

  • How scalable is UCROO?

    UCROO's cloud infrastructure has been built to rapidly and automatically adjust to meet growing, fluctuating and unpredicted usage.

  • What data/analytics does UCROO provide?

    As a university subscriber, the university is provided with a UCROO Analytics Centre for its network.

    The Analytics Centre provides behavioural data on students and staff using your UCROO network; offering new insights into connection, engagement and support at the university.

    Analytics can be compared across faculties, campuses, modes of study, user type and year levels. All analytics are exportable.

  • Why should my university use UCROO?

    Fundamental shifts in Higher Education are affecting traditional universities' ability to provide a quality student experience and retain students.

    Student:staff ratios are widening at a higher rate than ever before - making it increasingly difficult to support individual students adequately.

    Online content delivery - while offering learning flexibility and cutting costs - continues to disconnect students from their university and each other.

    UCROO helps universities adapt by implementing social technology to better connect, support and engage students from enrolment to graduation.

  • When should a student start using UCROO?

    We recommend universities invite students to join their UCROO network from enrolment.

  • How much does UCROO cost?

    UCROO provides a low-cost, competitively priced solution for improving student experience and retention at your university.

    We charge yearly subscriptions based only on the students and staff at your university using UCROO.

    UCROO has been built to work seamlessly across all modern desktop, laptop, tablet and mobile devices.

    UCROO is also accessible via native iPhone and Android applications.

    • OS X Snow Leopard+

    • Safari 4.0+
    • Chrome 14.0+

    Mac

  • How do users control their privacy settings on UCROO?

    Users control the collection, correction, settings and/or deletion of profile information from their profile page and privacy settings.

    Students can also hide themselves from network search and other features should they wish to.

    For further privacy information please visit UCROO Privacy Policy

  • What measures are taken to ensure the privacy and security of user information?

    We take privacy and security very seriously and make a sustained effort to guarantee the safety of the information that we hold, through both virtual and physical security processes.

    We have extensive virtual security measures, such as not storing sensitive information in plain text, penetration testing all proprietary code and ensuring servers can only be accessed via secure and authenticated connections.

    Our employees are thoroughly trained and provided with supporting policies and procedures to safeguard against social engineering. UCROO developers are required to work with 'anonimised' user information on all pre-production servers.

    Furthermore, our servers are physically secured in locked and alarmed buildings with uninterruptable power supplies at the Rackspace Data Centre in Sydney, Australia.

    For more information, visit Rackspace.

  • Can a UCROO university network be customised?

    This depends on the size and scope of the desired customisations - however, we are always open to hearing new ideas for a particular university's network.

    Keep in mind that we work closely with university subscribers to continually improve the product based on their feedback.

  • How much administration time does UCROO require?

    UCROO has been built to minimise active administration time, while maintaining a high-level of network moderation.

    Average estimated administration time per day: 5-10 minutes

  • What support is available to university staff?

    UCROO provides universities with enterprise level support.

    We provide access to a dedicated UCROO University Support Team Member, detailed documentation and best practice case studies to ensure the successful implementation and maintenance of your university network.

    To ensure smooth on-boarding and higher staff engagement, support webinars and a dedicated UCROO Resource Centre are available to all staff at the university.

    All university staff also have access to dedicated email and telephone support during business hours.

  • What integrations are possible with UCROO?

    Information System Integration

    Universities can connect selected student and staff information with UCROO via the UCROO API.

    Connecting student and staff information with UCROO means:

    • Students and staff do not have to enter profile information when signing up (saves time & increases sign ups)
    • Student and staff information stays up-to-date automatically
    • User management is administered automatically

    Single Sign On Integration (University/Student Portal)

    UCROO supports Single Sign On integration so that students do not have to log in again to access UCROO once they have already signed into another university system.

    UCROO's Single Sign On integration is generally implemented within University/Student Portals.

    Learning Tool Interoperability (LMS Integration)

    UCROO can be integrated with the university's Learning Management System via the Learning Tool Interoperability (LTI) standard.

    LTI allows unit coordinators/lecturers to embed UCROO as a class discussion tool within their subject on the LMS.

    Requirement: LTI must be enabled by the university. For more information visit: http://www.imsglobal.org

  • How much downtime does UCROO experience?

    As a cloud hosted Software as a Service solution, we push feature updates and improvements as they are developed, which guarantees maximum possible uptime for users (99.9%).

    Should downtime be required, we will endeavor to ensure these times occur during relatively low periods of usage and will communicate these well in advance.

  • Where does UCROO fit with respect to other technologies used by students?

    Learning Management Systems (LMSs)

    LMSs are educational platforms primarily used for an instructor's administrative purposes like lecture slides and grading.

    UCROO is a holistic student experience and retention technology that connects students across all aspects of their university lives - educational, social, personal (support) and professional - from enrolment to graduation.

    UCROO is primarily designed for interactive rather administrative purposes and has been built from the ground up to support user-generated exchanges, connections and community building.

    Facebook/Google+/Twitter

    Facebook, Google+ and Twitter are public social networks designed to connect users with people and content more efficiently.

    While UCROO also utilises social networking technology, the key differences between UCROO and Facebook/Google+/Twitter are:

    • UCROO is only accessible to students and staff at the university
    • UCROO is designed specifically for universities and its students
    • Students are more comfortable interacting with staff on UCROO
    • There is no advertising on UCROO
    • UCROO provides universities with insightful behavioural data
    • Universities control their UCROO network
    • UCROO integrates with university technologies
